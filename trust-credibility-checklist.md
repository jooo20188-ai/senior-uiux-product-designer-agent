# Trust and Credibility Checklist

Purpose: Check whether a site gives a visitor enough real, verifiable signals to trust it with money or personal data.

How to use: Used by Mode 4 and Mode 2 Full Audit, and any review of a business that asks for money or personal data (see [modes.md](./modes.md)). Each item is pass or fail. Mark N/A honestly when an item does not apply. Any unchecked item becomes a finding with a severity: Critical, High Impact, Medium Impact, or Polish.

## Identity
- [ ] A real business name is shown.
- [ ] Who is behind the product is findable.
- [ ] A physical location is given where relevant, such as a local business.
- [ ] The About page says something verifiable, not mission fog.
- [ ] The same business name appears consistently across pages and the footer.
- [ ] Team or founder photos, where shown, look like real people, not stock.
- [ ] A registration or tax identifier is shown where the jurisdiction expects it.

## Contact
- [ ] A working contact method is visible without hunting.
- [ ] A response expectation is stated, such as reply within one business day.
- [ ] A phone number is present for local and high-trust services.
- [ ] An address with a map is present for physical locations.
- [ ] A support path exists for existing customers, not only sales enquiries.
- [ ] Opening hours or availability are stated where they matter.

## Proof
- [ ] Testimonials are specific and plausible, with names or roles where permitted.
- [ ] Case studies or work examples are shown where relevant.
- [ ] Review platform links are present where they exist.
- [ ] Certifications and memberships link to their source.
- [ ] The portfolio shows real work, not stock mockups.
- [ ] Logos of clients or partners are used only with permission and are real.
- [ ] Any star rating links to the reviews it summarizes.
- [ ] Both positive and critical reviews are visible, not only glowing ones.
- [ ] Awards or press mentions link to the original source.
- [ ] Proof is dated so the visitor can judge how current it is.

## Policies and legal
- [ ] A privacy policy is present and linked from forms.
- [ ] Terms are findable.
- [ ] Refund and cancellation policy is written in plain language.
- [ ] Pricing discloses taxes and fees where applicable.
- [ ] Cookie and tracking consent is handled clearly, not buried.
- [ ] Policy pages show a last-updated date.

## Security and data cues
- [ ] HTTPS is used everywhere.
- [ ] Payment security is explained at the point of payment.
- [ ] Sensitive-data fields explain why data is needed and how it is stored.
- [ ] Third-party payment logos appear only where genuinely used.
- [ ] Accepted payment methods are shown before the final step.
- [ ] Account or password guidance is clear without demanding odd rules.

## Content honesty
- [ ] No exaggerated or unverifiable statistics.
- [ ] No fake-looking urgency.
- [ ] No generic stock photos posing as the team or premises.
- [ ] Content that ages, such as blog posts and reviews, shows dates.
- [ ] Claims of scale match the visible evidence.
- [ ] Superlatives such as "best" or "number one" are backed by a cited source.
- [ ] Product images match what the customer actually receives.

## Design signals
- [ ] No broken links or placeholder text such as lorem ipsum.
- [ ] Spelling and grammar are consistent.
- [ ] A favicon and proper page titles are present.
- [ ] Error pages are designed, not raw server output.
- [ ] The copyright year is current.
- [ ] Images load rather than showing broken-image icons.
- [ ] Contact and social links point to live, active destinations.

## Category notes
| Category | Trust signals to prioritize |
| --- | --- |
| Local business | Address, opening hours, phone, and reviews |
| Ecommerce | Returns, shipping, and total cost shown early |
| SaaS | Pricing, security, and data policies |
| Finance and health | Regulatory identity and cautious, qualified claims |

## Common Critical failures
- [ ] No real business identity anywhere on the site.
- [ ] No working contact method for a business that takes payment.
- [ ] Missing privacy policy while the site collects personal data.
- [ ] No HTTPS on pages that carry personal or payment data.
- [ ] Payment requested with no security explanation at the point of payment.
- [ ] Fabricated statistics or testimonials that misrepresent the business.
- [ ] Stock photos presented as the real team or premises.
